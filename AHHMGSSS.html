About Our School

Art. Hachalu Hundessa Memorial Gimbi Special Secondary School — Main Building

Establishment

Art. Hachalu Hundessa Memorial Gimbi Special Secondary School was established in 2013 E.C. in Oromia Regional State, West Wollega Zone, Gimbi Town. The school has provided teaching to 144 students from its establishment to the present.

School Mission

To produce competent, adaptable, innovative manpower that ensures job creation on the basis of outcome-based education by involving stakeholders and the business community, enhancing socio-economic development of Oromia and Ethiopia.

Key Activities

  • Building Technology Array
  • Supporting Small Scale Micro Enterprise
  • Technology Accumulation and Transfer
  • Producing Competent Industry Manpower
  • Capacity Building of the School

Vision

To become a centre of excellence offering accredited, high-quality, cutting-edge technology training through multi-site campuses.

Values

Equality & Equity · Academic Freedom · Corporate Social Responsibility · Trusted Partnership · Participatory Teamwork · Client Satisfaction · Efficiency · Credibility · Competence · Professional Integrity · Self-initiation · Commitment to Excellence

45 Common Student Questions

Click any question to reveal the answer.

1. How do I create an effective study timetable?

List all subjects. Allocate more time to difficult topics. Use 45-minute study blocks with 10-minute breaks. Include weekends for revision and update every two weeks.

2. Best way to prepare for national exams?

Begin 3–4 months early. Collect past papers. Practice under timed conditions. Focus on understanding concepts. Join a study group. Review mistakes carefully.

3. How can I improve my memory for exams?

Use active recall — test yourself. Create mind maps. Teach material to someone else. Use mnemonics. Get enough sleep — sleep consolidates memory.

4. What should I do the night before an exam?

Review summary notes lightly. Prepare everything needed. Eat a healthy dinner. Go to bed early. Avoid screens before sleep. Visualize success.

5. How do I manage exam stress?

Practice deep breathing. Exercise regularly. Break study into small tasks. Talk to friends or a counselor. Avoid comparing yourself. One exam does not define your worth.

6. How can I find scholarship opportunities?

Check government education websites, university portals, NGO announcements. Visit school guidance office. Search online platforms. Follow Ethiopian education pages.

7. What do scholarship committees look for?

Strong academic record, leadership potential, community service, clear career goals, compelling personal story. They want students who will give back to their community.

8. How to write a winning scholarship essay?

Start with a powerful personal story. Explain challenges overcome. Connect goals to community needs. Be specific, honest, passionate. Ask a teacher to review.

9. What documents needed for scholarship?

Academic transcripts, recommendation letters (2–3), personal statement, CV, proof of identity, sometimes proof of financial need. Keep scanned copies ready.

10. How early should I apply for scholarships?

Start 6–12 months before enrollment. Many international deadlines are 8–10 months before the academic year. Apply to multiple opportunities.

11. Best study techniques for science?

Draw diagrams. Solve numerical problems daily. Understand formulas. Conduct simple experiments. Use flashcards. Watch educational videos for complex topics.

12. How to prepare for mathematics exams?

Practice every day. Start easy and increase difficulty. Write all steps. Learn from mistakes. Memorize key formulas but understand derivations.

13. How can I improve English skills?

Read English books and newspapers daily. Watch programs with subtitles. Practice speaking with friends. Keep a vocabulary notebook. Write essays and ask for feedback.

14. What is the Pomodoro Technique?

Study 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. After four cycles, take a longer break. Use the timer above to practice this proven technique.

15. How do I stay motivated?

Set small daily goals. Reward yourself. Remind yourself of long-term dreams. Study with motivated friends. Change environment occasionally. Track progress.

16. What foods help with studying?

Protein-rich foods, fruits (bananas, berries), nuts, whole grains. Drink plenty of water. Avoid heavy oily meals. Limit sugar and caffeine.

17. How much sleep do I need?

Aim for 7–8 hours every night. Sleep consolidates learning. All-nighters reduce performance. Maintain a consistent sleep schedule.

18. How to handle difficult subjects?

Find a real-world connection. Break into small topics. Ask a classmate to explain differently. Use YouTube tutorials. Reward yourself after each session.

19. Best way to take notes?

Cornell method: divide page into notes, cues, summary. Write key points, not everything. Use abbreviations. Review and rewrite same day.

20. How to prepare for oral presentations?

Practice out loud. Record yourself. Prepare note cards. Anticipate questions. Arrive early, breathe deeply, speak slowly.

21. How to make group study effective?

Limit to 3–5 focused members. Set clear agenda. Each member teaches one topic. Quiz each other. Avoid distractions. End with summary.

22. What if I fail an exam?

Feel disappointed, then move forward. Analyze what went wrong. Talk to your teacher. Create a new study plan. Failure is a stepping stone.

23. How to balance school and family?

Create a weekly schedule. Communicate with family. Use early morning hours. Combine activities when possible.

24. Best online resources for Ethiopian students?

Ministry of Education website, Khan Academy, Coursera, YouTube educational channels, Ethiopian student Telegram forums.

25. How to choose the right university program?

Identify interests and strengths. Research job market demand. Talk to professionals. Consider long-term goals. Choose what fits you.

26. What is a growth mindset?

Belief that intelligence can be developed through effort. Embrace challenges, learn from criticism, persist through setbacks.

27. How to avoid procrastination?

Use the 2-minute rule. Break tasks into tiny steps. Remove distractions. Set earlier deadlines. Tell someone your goals for accountability.

28. How to improve handwriting?

Practice daily 10 minutes. Slow down. Use lined paper. Maintain consistent letter size. Legible handwriting earns extra marks.

29. What is the Feynman Technique?

Explain a concept simply as if teaching a child. Identify gaps. Go back to source. If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it.

30. Preparing for multiple exams in one week?

Create priority list. Study hardest subject first. Rotate subjects. Use summary sheets. Don't neglect any subject.

31. How can I find a mentor?

Look among teachers, older students, professionals. Approach respectfully with specific questions. Show commitment. Be appreciative.

32. Benefits of extracurricular activities?

Develop leadership, teamwork, time management. Strengthen scholarship applications. Reduce stress. Build friendships. Balance is key.

33. How to set SMART goals?

Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. Example: "Score above 80% in math by studying 1 hour daily."

34. How can parents support students?

Provide quiet study space. Ensure proper nutrition and sleep. Offer encouragement, not pressure. Celebrate effort. Avoid comparing.

35. What if I don't understand a topic?

Ask teacher immediately. Write down confusion. Ask classmate. Look online. Visit library. Never let confusion accumulate.

36. How to prepare a strong CV?

Include education, awards, volunteer work, skills. One page. Clear headings. Proofread. Ask teacher to review. Update every 6 months.

37. Importance of past exam papers?

Reveal exam format, question types, marking schemes. Help practice time management. Complete at least 5 years of past papers.

38. What should I do after completing each topic to prepare effectively for the entrance exam?

After each topic, create as many questions as you can—especially if it’s a key topic for the entrance exam—so you can test your understanding and spot weak spots.

39. Habits of top-performing students?

Consistent routines, ask questions, review regularly, sleep well, exercise, set goals, avoid cramming, seek help. Learning is a daily habit.

40. How to stay focused at home?

Designate study area. Keep it clean. Turn off notifications. Use website blockers. Tell family your schedule. Use instrumental music.

41. What to do during school holidays?

Rest first. Review difficult topics. Read outside curriculum. Learn a new skill. Volunteer. Prepare for upcoming topics. Balance relaxation with productivity.

42. How to handle negative feedback?

Listen without defensiveness. Separate emotions from feedback. Ask for specific suggestions. Thank the teacher. Create an action plan.

43. Best ways to revise before an exam?

Active recall. Summary mind maps from memory. Teach aloud. Solve past papers. Focus on weak areas. Review, don't re-learn.

44. How can technology help studies?

Educational apps, tutorial videos, online communities, cloud backup. Limit social media — it's the biggest distraction.

45. #1 factor for academic success?

Consistency. Studying a little every day beats cramming. Small efforts compound into massive results. Build daily habits. There is no shortcut.

Art. Hachalu Hundessa

1986 – June 29, 2020
Singer · Songwriter · Civil Rights Activist

Art. Hachalu Hundessa was a prominent Ethiopian singer, songwriter, and civil rights activist whose powerful music became the defining soundtrack for the Oromo people's struggle for political freedom and equality.

1986–2002: Early Life

Born in Ambo in 1986 to Hundessa Bonsa and Gudatu Hora. Named Haacaaluu, fifth of eight children. Spent youth tending cattle and singing.

2003–2008: Imprisonment

Arrested at 17 for Oromo self-rule protests. Five years in Karchale Ambo prison — composed nine songs and educated himself.

"Prison makes a man stronger — his father's words that shaped a revolutionary."

2009–2014: Rise to Stardom

Sanyii Mootii (2009) — immediate success. Married Fantanesh Demisse. Waa'ee Keenya (2013) — #1 African album on Amazon Music.

2015–2018: The Revolution

"Maalan Jiraa?" (2015) became the Oromo protest anthem, helping force regime change in 2018.

June 2020: Assassination

Shot June 29, 2020 in Addis Ababa at age 34. Nationwide unrest — 160+ deaths.

Legacy

Buried at Emmanuel Orthodox Church, Ambo. Shooter received life imprisonment. Maal Mallisaa released posthumously (2021).
